Biography

Welcome to my webpage! I'm Huilin Tai, a fourth year bechlor student in Statistics and Computer Science at McGill University. I specialize in machine learning and data science, with a strong foundation in programming languages like Python, Java, C, R, and Ocaml.

My research journey has been a path filled with diverse and enriching experiences. It spans from theoretical computer science, with a focus on algorithm design in communication theory and the exploration of replicability in agnostic learning, to the practical application of machine learning in visual odometry, cell profiling, and spatial gene expression prediction. Additionally, my experience in statistical modeling for immunology research and survival analysis strengthened my skills in developing robust analytical frameworks and gave me opportunities to collaborate with brilliant researchers across various fields.

I also actively participate in academic support as CS-helpdesk tutor and course assistant for courses such as Algebra, Probability, and Statistical Computation. This webpage is a glimpse into my academic and research interests - a space where I share my explorations and experiences in the captivating world of data and computation.

Research Experience

McGill University Department of Computer Science

Excess-Error Dependent Replicability (Jan 2024 - present, supervised by Professor Hatami): Explored replicability in agnostic learning with a focus on empirical data-based error approximation.

Unsupervised Learning for Cell Profiling (Jan. 2023 - Jan. 2024, Ding Lab): Employed unsupervised models for accurate cell profiling and transcriptional signature identification in PBMCs.

Machine Learning Application for Sex Prediction (Nov. 2022 - April. 2024, Ding Lab): Developed machine learning classifiers for sex prediction based on gene expressions, achieving high accuracy with advanced feature selection and neural network optimization.

Deep Learning Model for Spatial Gene Expression Prediction (June. 2023 - Sep. 2023, Ding Lab): Designed a Variational Autoencoder for spatial gene expression prediction, implementing novel visualization methods for genomic feature interaction in tissue space.

University at Buffalo

McGill University Department of Mathematics and Statistics

NP Binding Process Quantification (Nov. 2022 - April. 2023, Khadra Lab): Applied probability models to analyze nanoparticle binding dynamics in T-cell activation and therapy research.
