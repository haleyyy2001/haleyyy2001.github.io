About Me

I'm a Master's student in Computer Science at Columbia University (GPA: 4.0), with a Bachelor's degree in Statistics and Computer Science from McGill University (GPA: 3.86).

My research spans from developing production-scale genomic classification pipelines and benchmarking temporal dynamics in text-to-video generation, to creating novel computational tools for single-cell analysis. I've published 6 papers in prestigious journals including Cell Reports Methods, with work accepted at ICML 2025 and ACM Multimedia.

Currently, I work as an ML Research Assistant at Columbia University under Professor Mohammed AlQuraishi, focusing on deep learning benchmarking for antimicrobial resistance prediction. My industry experience includes data science internships at The Feinstein Institutes and positions developing real-time optimization systems.
